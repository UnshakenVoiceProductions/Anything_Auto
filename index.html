Common codes, symptoms, and diagnostics

These examples give customers a simple starting point before scheduling a proper inspection.

🧪

P0101

Mass air flow performance issues can affect drivability, fuel trims, idle quality, and acceleration. Testing is usually better than guessing parts.

⛽

P2022

Fuel level sensor range or performance issues can affect gauge accuracy and fuel-system reporting. It may not be the root cause of another symptom, but it still needs attention.

🛞

Vibration, wobble, or clicking

These symptoms can come from tires, front-end parts, brakes, wheel bearings, CV joints, or alignment-related issues. The speed and conditions matter.

🚨

Check engine light

A code points in a direction, but proper testing is still needed before recommending parts or repairs.

Frequently asked questions

Sample questions with simple answers for first-time visitors.

My vehicle shakes at highway speed. What should I mention when I contact the shop?

Include the speed range, whether it changes during braking or turning, and whether the steering wheel shakes or the whole vehicle vibrates.

If my check engine light is on, should I still drive it?

If the light is flashing, the vehicle is running poorly, or the problem feels unsafe, limit driving and have it checked as soon as possible.

Can I ask a question before I schedule?

Yes. Start with Ask Chris for a first-pass answer. If you still need direct help, you can send the question to Chris for follow-up.

What helps the shop diagnose a problem faster?

Recent repairs, warning lights, when the issue happens, unusual noises, smells, temperature changes, and whether the problem is constant or intermittent.
