Common codes, issues, and diagnostics

These examples are meant to help customers understand what certain codes or symptoms may point toward before a proper inspection is scheduled.

🧪

P0101

Mass Air Flow Circuit Range/Performance Problem.

  • May affect idle quality, drivability, and fuel trims.
  • Can be caused by intake leaks, sensor issues, contamination, or related airflow problems.
  • Testing is better than guessing parts.
⛽

P2022

Fuel Level Sensor Circuit Range/Performance Problem.

  • Can affect gauge accuracy and fuel-level reporting.
  • May not be the cause of another drivability symptom, but it still deserves attention.
  • Inspection helps confirm whether the fault is sensor, wiring, or module related.
🛞

Wobble, vibration, or clicking

These symptoms often need condition-based troubleshooting.

  • Possible causes include tires, balance, alignment, wheel bearings, CV joints, brake issues, or front-end parts.
  • Speed range and whether it happens while braking or turning matters.
  • A road test and physical inspection are often the best next step.
🚨

Check engine light

A stored code gives direction, not automatic certainty.

  • Some faults are minor; others can affect safety, fuel economy, or drivability.
  • A flashing light or severe misfire should be treated more urgently.
  • Ask Chris for a first pass, then schedule testing if needed.

Frequently asked questions

Simple sample questions with straightforward answers for first-time visitors.

Do I need an appointment for diagnostics?

It is best to request service first so the shop can plan the right amount of time and understand the problem before you arrive.

What should I tell the shop if my vehicle shakes on the highway?

Share the speed range, whether it changes while braking or turning, and whether the shaking is in the steering wheel, seat, or whole vehicle.

Can I ask a question before I bring the vehicle in?

Yes. Use Ask Chris for a first-pass answer. If you still need direct help, you can send the question to Chris for follow-up.

Do you inspect motorcycles and trailers?

Yes. The public business information indicates the shop is certified for Pennsylvania motorcycle inspections and annual trailer inspections.

What vehicles does the shop work on?

The shop works on a wide variety of consumer vehicles from small sedans to medium-duty trucks, but not large big-rig heavy commercial trucks or heavy industrial trailers.

What helps the shop diagnose a problem faster?

Warning lights, when the problem happens, what recent repairs were done, and any unusual noises, smells, smoke, or vibration all help.
